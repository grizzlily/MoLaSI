Idioms about winter
Idioms about winter

As cool as a cucumber – спокійна стійка до стресів людина
She never reacts to stressful situations she's always as cool as a cucumber.


As cold as ice – дуже холодна людина
What can be as cold as ice. I sometimes it seems like he never smiles.


Fits like a glove! - дуже добре підходити
Wow! These are the best shoes I have ever worn. They fit like a glove!


Bolt from the blue — як сніг на голову.
The news of her marriage was a bolt from the blue for everyone.


Get cold feet - не зважитися на щось в останню мить.
The burglar got cold feet when the dog started barking.


Be snowed under - бути завантаженим, заваленим роботою
I’m absolutely snowed under with work at the moment.


When hell freezes over - «Коли рак на горі свисне»: щось, що навряд чи станеться.
I guess, after everything you have done, she will forgive you when hell freezes over.


Break the ice - розтопити кригу, розрядити ситуацію.
You need an event to help break the ice and help make new friends.


Be chilled to the bone - замерзнути до мозку кісток.
Walking home in the snow, we got chilled to the bone.


Walk on thin ice - «грати з вогнем», «ходити тонкою кригою».
You’re walking on thin ice by continuing to come in late. If the boss notices, you’ll be fired.


Bundle up - одягатися тепліше, закутатися
You’d better bundle up because it’s below freezing outside.


Snowball’s chance in hell - мало шансів на успіх, як сніжка в пеклі
I knew it was a snowball’s chance in hell to see her but I went to the bar.
